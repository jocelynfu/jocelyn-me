# State Share

*Share a link. Your friend jumps into the exact game moment you were just playing.*

## What it is

State Share was a Stadia-only feature — something genuinely impossible on any other gaming platform at the time. Because the game ran in the cloud, we could capture the *full game state* — world state, player position, inventory — and encode it into a shareable link. Anyone who opened the link jumped straight into that exact moment.

Beat an impossible boss and want your friends to try? Share the fight. Set up a devious speed-run challenge? Share the starting line. One player's moment became something an entire community could pick up and build on.

## The showcase

State Share was showcased at **Google's GDC 2019 keynote**, presented by Q-Games' Dylan Cuthbert, and picked up by press including Polygon, GameSpot, and TechRaptor.

## My role

I owned Stadia's sharing team and led State Share as one of its flagship 0→1 features — working across game developers (who had to define what "state" meant for their games) and platform engineering (who had to make capturing and restoring it seamless).

## Why I still love it

Most sharing features share a *recording* of an experience. State Share shared the experience itself. It remains the purest example I've worked on of a platform capability unlocking a fundamentally new kind of social product.
