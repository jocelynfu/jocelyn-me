# Webflow (2024–present)

**Staff Product Manager, AI** · Remote from Boston

Webflow is an enterprise-grade web platform — the visual way to design, build, and publish real production websites. I was originally hired for traditional growth work, but during the interview process I proposed expanding my own scope to include Webflow's early AI efforts. That bet became the core of my job.

## What I do

- **AI products, 0 → 1.** I proposed and led Webflow's first end-to-end AI product ([AI Site Builder](ai-site-builder.md)) and its first truly agentic product ([AI Assistant](ai-assistant.md)). This is hands-on AI PM work: harness engineering, LLM evaluations, agentic workflows, and in-product UX — not just writing requirements.
- **Growth.** I also led a separate growth engineering team optimizing the full user funnel — acquisition (AI landing pages), activation (AI-first first-time-user experience), and retention (churn prevention). Experiments drove $2M+ in additional ARR.
- **AI monetization.** Piloted Webflow's usage-based AI monetization approach and co-lead the company's AI strategy working group.

## Key projects

| Project | One-liner |
|---|---|
| [AI Site Builder](ai-site-builder.md) | Describe your site, and AI builds it. $5M+ ARR in under a year. |
| [AI Assistant](ai-assistant.md) | A conversational agent that designs and edits live Webflow sites. Demoed in the Webflow Conf 2025 opening keynote. |

## What this chapter taught me

The job of an AI PM isn't the old "define requirements and ship" model — it's continuously re-scoping what's possible as the underlying models evolve, and redesigning the product around it. Constraints that are a necessary safety net one year become a cage the next.
