# SaaS Plan Consolidation

*From a dozen-plus confusing plans to three clear ones — first for Square Online, then across all of Square.*

📰 Public coverage: [Inside Square's new pricing plans](https://squareup.com/us/en/the-bottom-line/inside-square/pricing-plans)

## The problem

Square Online inherited a pricing mess from the Weebly acquisition: 12+ plans, prices up to 65% below competitors for similar features, a free plan generous enough that nearly half of the highest-value sellers never needed to upgrade, and plans that weren't tailored to Square's strongest segment (food & beverage). Sales couldn't pitch the plans; support couldn't explain them.

## What I did

I led the redesign end-to-end over more than a year:

1. **Research first.** Conjoint analysis and qualitative seller interviews before touching a single price.
2. **Simplify.** Consolidated to 3 plans (Free + two paid tiers), raised prices while staying below competitors, deliberately trimmed the free plan, and moved F&B value features into the paid tiers.
3. **De-risk the rollout.** Phased launch — new sellers first to validate assumptions, then existing sellers with conservative churn projections modeled to ensure at least breakeven.

The hardest call was weakening the free plan, where most active sellers sat. The bet was that a genuinely stronger paid tier was better for them — even if the transition was uncomfortable. It paid off: churn came in well below projections.

I also cared a lot about doing this honestly. We redesigned the downgrade flow to *surface* each seller's feature usage so they could make informed decisions, and made downgrading easier, not harder. Retention improved anyway — because of that trust, not despite it.

## Results

- **50% YoY SaaS revenue growth** for Square Online (repricing plus follow-up funnel experiments)
- Higher ARPU and subscriber growth among both new and existing sellers, with churn below projections

## The sequel

On the strength of the Square Online results, Square's CPO selected me to lead the company-wide pricing redesign: **consolidating 19 subscription plans into 3** across the whole SaaS portfolio, partnering with finance, legal, and 50+ engineers across 8 product teams. That work became the simplified Free / Plus / Premium structure Square later launched publicly.
