# Customer Accounts for Square Online

*Turning anonymous transactions into real customer relationships.*

📰 Public docs: [Customer accounts for Square Online](https://squareup.com/help/us/en/article/7802-customer-accounts-for-square-online) · [Launch announcement](https://community.squareup.com/t5/Archived-Articles-Read-Only/New-Customer-accounts-for-Square-Online/ba-p/381006)

## The problem

Before customer accounts, buyer identity at Square was fragmented: offline purchases were tied to a credit card, online purchases to an email, and every merchant had a separate view of the same customer. Buyers were effectively anonymous from one transaction to the next.

## The insight

The original scope was modest: accounts for a merchant's online store only. But research with *both* sides — merchants and buyers — changed the plan. Merchants wanted to know and reach their customers regardless of channel. Buyers wanted easy checkout, loyalty, and coupons. Two different user groups, quietly pointing at the same missing foundation: a **shared account platform across online and offline**, not an online-only feature.

The strongest research insights usually aren't feature requests — they're when different users' needs converge on the same gap.

## What I built

I championed expanding the scope and drove a cross-team collaboration with the customer platform and data-pipeline teams to unify buyer identity: tying offline and online transactions to the same buyer, across merchants. For buyers, that meant order tracking, order history, easy reordering, and saved payment info for faster checkout. For merchants, it meant actually knowing their customers — and a foundation for loyalty, gift cards, and discounts, which all depend on knowing which buyer is which.

## Results

- **64% adoption** by active users
- **12% lift in seller annual revenue retention** — the **#1 retention driver** at Square Online
- A genuinely unified experience for both merchants and buyers, and the foundation that later personalization features built on
