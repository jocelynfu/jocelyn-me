# Pricing Strategy — F500 B2B Device Company

*Finding the revenue hiding in the pricing data.*

## The engagement

A Fortune 500 B2B device company suspected it was leaving money on the table in how it priced and launched products.

## What I did

- **Collected and analyzed product pricing data** across the portfolio to identify **$20–30M of revenue opportunity** — pricing inconsistencies and under-priced segments the client couldn't see without pulling the data together.
- **Diagnosed the product launch process** and proposed a launch transformation to fix the root cause, so new products wouldn't recreate the same pricing gaps.

## What stuck with me

Pricing problems are usually process problems wearing a trench coat. The $20–30M wasn't a spreadsheet trick — it was the visible symptom of a launch process that set prices once and never revisited them. That instinct (fix the process, not just the number) showed up again years later in my pricing work at Square.
